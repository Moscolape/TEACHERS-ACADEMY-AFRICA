BACKGROUND: THE PROBLEM/NEED

The world is actively moving towards achieving Sustainable Development Goal 4 (SDG 4). However, there are still more than 260 million out-of-school children and more than 600 million in school without the basic skills, knowledge, and values required to thrive now and in the future.

(UN: The Education Commission Report, 2019).

To meet the Sustainable Development Goal 4 by 2030, an estimated 19.6 million teachers will be needed in Africa.

(UNESCO Institute for Statistics)

Teacher Experience is the most important determinant factor for learning outcomes at the school level. In many countries, teachers are not only short in supply, they are mostly isolated, poorly trained, and not supported to provide effective teaching and learning. Even where there are teachers, many are poorly trained or unqualified and often lack pedagogical and subject knowledge.

(UN: The Education Commission Report, 2019).
